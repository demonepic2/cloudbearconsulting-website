## Copywriting: Words That Convert

### Headlines That Work Now

**Formula 1: Outcome + Timeframe**
> "Get [Specific Result] in [Timeframe] without [Pain Point]"

**Formula 2: Problem Agitation**
> "Stop [Painful Thing] — Start [Desired Outcome]"

**Formula 3: Quantified Value**
> "Save [X Hours/Dollars] Every [Week/Month] with [Product]"

**What's working in 2025:**
- Specificity beats cleverness every time
- Numbers increase believability (not "fast" but "in 2.3 seconds")
- Headlines that pass the "5-second test" — can a stranger explain your offer after 5 seconds?

### The Headline Hierarchy

| Element | Purpose | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| H1 Headline | State the primary benefit | 50-60 characters |
| Subheadline | Expand on HOW or WHO | 1-2 sentences |
| Supporting text | Build desire, address doubt | 2-3 short sentences |

### Body Copy Rules

1. **Write at 6th-grade level.** Complexity kills conversions. Use Hemingway App.
2. **Benefits > Features.** Nobody cares about "AI-powered." They care about "save 4 hours/week."
3. **Second person only.** "You" and "your" — never "we" or "our" in benefit statements.
4. **Short paragraphs.** 2-3 sentences max. White space is your friend.
5. **Front-load value.** First sentence of every section should deliver value.

### CTA Copy That Converts

**Stop using:**
- "Submit" (conversion killer)
- "Click here" (vague)
- "Learn more" (no outcome)
- "Sign up" (implies commitment)

**Start using:**
- "Get My Free [Thing]" — ownership language
- "Start My Trial" — implies temporary, low-risk
- "Create My [Outcome]" — action + result
- "See It In Action" — curiosity + specificity
- "Claim My Spot" — scarcity + ownership

**Proven tactic:** Changing "Sign up for free" → "Trial for free" increased conversions 104% in one test. "Trial" implies temporary evaluation; "Sign up" implies ongoing commitment.

## Visual Design: The Science of Attention

### Visual Hierarchy Checklist

Users scan in **Z-pattern** (visual pages) or **F-pattern** (text-heavy pages). Design accordingly:

1. **Size = Importance.** Headline largest, CTA button prominent, supporting text smaller.
2. **Contrast = Attention.** CTA must pop against background.
3. **White space = Clarity.** Cramped layouts kill conversions.
4. **Directional cues.** Use arrows, eye gaze, or visual flow pointing toward CTA.

### The Squint Test
Blur your eyes and look at your page. The elements that stand out should be:
1. Your headline
2. Your CTA button
3. Your hero image

If anything else stands out first, redesign.

### Images That Convert

**Use:**
- ✅ Product in action (real screenshots, demos)
- ✅ Real customer photos in testimonials (not stock)
- ✅ Team photos (builds human connection)
- ✅ Before/after visuals
- ✅ Short explainer videos (can lift conversions up to 86%)

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Generic stock photos (erodes trust instantly)
- ❌ Decorative images that don't support the message
- ❌ Cluttered visuals competing with your CTA
- ❌ Low-quality or pixelated images

**Pro tip:** If you show a person, position their eye gaze toward your CTA or key copy. Users follow eye direction.

## Colors & CTA Buttons: What Actually Works

### The Truth About Button Colors

**There is no "best" color.** The research is clear: what matters is **contrast**, not the specific hue.

However, here's what performs well across studies:
- **Orange:** 32-40% higher click rates (urgency + friendliness)
- **Red:** Creates urgency, works for "Buy Now" scenarios
- **Green:** Works for eco/health brands, "Start" actions
- **Blue:** Trust, reliability — but only if it contrasts with your page

### Button Design Specs

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Size | Minimum 44x44 pixels (thumb-friendly) |
| Contrast | Must pass WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum ratio) |
| Padding | Generous — button shouldn't feel cramped |
| Shape | Slight rounding performs better than sharp corners |
| Shadow | Subtle shadow adds depth and clickability |

### Color Psychology Quick Reference

| Color | Association | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Orange | Energy, urgency, action | Buy now, Get started |
| Red | Urgency, excitement, power | Limited time, Sales |
| Green | Growth, safety, go | Free trials, Sign ups |
| Blue | Trust, reliability, calm | Professional services |
| Black | Luxury, exclusivity | Premium offers |
| Yellow | Optimism, attention | Highlighting, Accents |

### The Real Rule

**Your CTA button should be the single most visually prominent element on the page.** If it's not, change the color, size, or surrounding contrast until it is.

## Social Proof: Placement & Format

### Where to Place Social Proof

| Location | Type of Proof | Purpose |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Above fold | Client logos (3-5) | Instant credibility |
| Below hero | User count / stats | "Used by 50,000+ teams" |
| After benefits | Testimonials (2-3) | Validate claims |
| Near CTA | Trust badges | Reduce purchase anxiety |
| Checkout/form | Security seals | Address security concerns |

### Social Proof That Converts

**Tier 1 (Highest Impact):**
- Video testimonials (20-60 seconds)
- Case studies with specific metrics
- Before/after results with numbers

**Tier 2 (High Impact):**
- Written testimonials with full name + photo + title
- Star ratings from third-party platforms (G2, Capterra, Google)
- Client logos (recognizable brands only)

**Tier 3 (Good to Have):**
- User counts ("Join 50,000+ marketers")
- Press mentions/media logos
- Awards and certifications

### Testimonial Formula

The best testimonials follow this structure:
> **[Specific problem before] → [Specific solution/result] → [Quantified outcome]**

Example:
> "We were wasting 15 hours/week on manual reporting. [Product] automated everything, and now we've cut that to 30 minutes. Our team recovered 14+ hours every single week." — Jane Smith, Marketing Director, Acme Corp

**Critical:** Include full name, title, company, and photo. Anonymous testimonials convert 68% worse than identified ones.

## Forms: The Conversion Chokepoint

### The Form Field Formula

| # of Fields | Use Case | Conversion Impact |
|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| 2-3 | Top of funnel (newsletter, lead magnet) | Highest conversion |
| 4-5 | Mid funnel (demo request, trial signup) | Optimal balance |
| 6+ | Bottom funnel only (quotes, applications) | Use only when necessary |

**The data is unambiguous:** Reducing form fields from 11 to 4 increases conversions by 160%.

### Form Design Best Practices

1. **Single-column layout only.** Multi-column forms confuse users.
2. **Top-aligned labels.** Easier to scan than left-aligned.
3. **Inline validation.** Show errors immediately, not after submit.
4. **Progress indicators.** If multi-step, show "Step 1 of 3."
5. **Smart defaults.** Pre-fill country, use dropdowns for predictable inputs.
6. **Descriptive placeholders.** "name@company.com" not "Enter email."

### What to Cut

If you're not using the data in your first follow-up, don't ask for it:

- ❌ Company size (ask later)
- ❌ Phone number (unless calling is your workflow)
- ❌ Full address (unless shipping)
- ❌ "How did you hear about us?" (ask post-conversion)
- ❌ CAPTCHA (use honeypot instead)

### The Two-Step Form Technique

Some companies report **5x conversion increases** using two-step forms:

**Step 1:** Capture email + primary need (low commitment)
**Step 2:** Ask for detailed info after initial conversion

This leverages the "endowed progress effect" — users who start are more likely to finish.

## Mobile Optimization: Non-Negotiable

### Mobile Stats You Can't Ignore
- 83% of landing page traffic is mobile
- Mobile converts 8% lower than desktop (fix this gap!)
- 53% abandon pages taking >3 seconds to load
- 50% of landing pages still aren't mobile-optimized

### Mobile Design Specifications

| Element | Mobile Spec |
|---------|-------------|
| Font size | Minimum 16px body text |
| Buttons | Minimum 44x44px tap targets |
| Form fields | Full-width, stacked vertically |
| Images | Compressed, responsive, lazy-loaded |
| Layout | Single-column only |
| CTA | Sticky bottom bar or prominently visible |

### The "Arm's Length" Test

Hold your phone at arm's length. Can you:
- Read all text clearly?
- Tap buttons without zooming?
- Complete the form without frustration?

If not, redesign for mobile first.

## Page Speed: Every Second Costs Money

### The Speed-Conversion Relationship

| Load Time | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| <2 seconds | Optimal (47% expect this) |
| 2-3 seconds | Acceptable |
| 3-5 seconds | Losing significant conversions |
| 5+ seconds | Emergency — fix immediately |

**Each additional second of load time = 7% conversion loss**

### Quick Speed Wins

| Action | Impact | Difficulty |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Compress images (WebP/AVIF) | 20-40% faster | Easy |
| Enable browser caching | 30-50% improvement | Easy |
| Use a CDN (Cloudflare free tier) | 30-50% faster globally | Easy |
| Minify CSS/JS | 10-20% faster | Medium |
| Lazy load images | Significant for long pages | Medium |
| Remove unused scripts | Varies | Medium |

### Test Your Speed
- Google PageSpeed Insights
- GTmetrix
- WebPageTest

**Target scores:**
- Mobile: 90+ Performance score
- Desktop: 95+ Performance score

## A/B Testing: What to Test First

### Priority Testing Order

Test in this sequence for maximum impact:

1. **Headline** — 27-104% lift potential
2. **CTA copy** — 104% lift documented (single word changes)
3. **Form length** — 120%+ lift from field reduction
4. **CTA button color/size** — Variable, but quick wins
5. **Social proof placement** — 10-30% lifts common
6. **Hero image/video** — Significant but slower to test

### Testing Rules

1. **Test one element at a time.** Otherwise, you can't attribute results.
2. **Let tests run to statistical significance.** Use Evan Miller's calculator.
3. **Don't peek.** Checking early leads to bad decisions.
4. **Document everything.** Build a knowledge base of what works for YOUR audience.
5. **Losing tests are still valuable.** They tell you what doesn't work.
